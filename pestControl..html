Pest Control Strategies

Learn integrated pest management, biological controls, and organic remedies to detect pests early and treat them effectively.

Pest prevention, monitoring, and treatment guidance for healthier crops.

Integrated Pest Management

Use a balanced approach to pest control that reduces chemical use and protects beneficial insects.

Monitoring

Regular field scouting helps identify pests early before damage spreads across the crop.

Inspect leaves, stems, and soil weekly.

Biological controls

Use natural predators, parasitoids, and beneficial microbes to reduce pest populations.

Encourage biodiversity and habitat for pollinators.

Organic remedies

Apply botanical oils, neem extracts, and safe traps to manage pests with low environmental impact.

Combine methods for greater effectiveness.

Pest detection and treatment

Act quickly to protect crop health and minimize yield loss with proper diagnosis and response.

Early detection tips

  • Check for leaf discoloration, holes, and wilting.
  • Watch for insect movement, webs, or sticky residues.
  • Use traps and pheromone lures to monitor pest pressure.

Effective treatment

  • Choose the least disruptive control first.
  • Apply treatments in the early morning or late evening.
  • Rotate control methods to avoid pest resistance.
