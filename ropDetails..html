Major crop categories

Choose your crop group and review the best practices for cultivation and harvest.

Grains

Rice, maize, wheat, and millet require balanced water, good seedbed preparation, and careful pest monitoring.

Soil: fertile, well-drained; Harvest: moisture-based timing.

Fruits

Fruit crops need deep soil, regular nutrition, and pruning to maintain healthy, high-yielding orchards.

Soil: loamy, rich organic matter; Harvest: graded by maturity.

Vegetables

Vegetables perform best with frequent irrigation, light feeding, and pest-safe protective practices.

Soil: fertile, loose; Harvest: frequent picking.

Crop profiles

Each profile includes cultivation tips, soil needs, nutrition, and common uses for each crop type.

Variety: Maize

  • Soil: warm, well-drained, fertile loam.
  • Water: regular moisture during tasseling and grain fill.
  • Use: food, feed, and industrial products.

Variety: Tomato

  • Soil: rich in organic matter, pH 6.0–6.8.
  • Care: staking, drip irrigation, and disease scouting.
  • Use: fresh market, sauces, and processing.

Variety: Mango

  • Soil: deep, fertile, well-drained, with good organic content.
  • Nutrition: balanced NPK and micronutrient support.
  • Use: fresh fruit, dried products, and export.

Variety: Cotton

  • Soil: loamy to sandy loam with good drainage.
  • Care: pest monitoring, timely irrigation, and harvest at boll maturity.
  • Use: fiber, seed oil, and byproducts.
